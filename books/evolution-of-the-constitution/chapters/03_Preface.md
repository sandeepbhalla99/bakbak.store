# Preface

The circle appears to be complete when a student ends up revising the very first book he started with in the first place. Late T.L. Venkatarama Aiyar's, a former Judge of Supreme Court of India had delivered a public lecture in 1967 on the subject of Constitution in 'Chimanlal Setalvad Law Lectures'  In precise words, he explained the evolution, working and nature of Constitution of India besides comparing the Fundamental rights in our Constitution with the Constitution of USA. These lectures were delivered in 1967 and were printed by University of Bombay in 1970 under the title 'Evolution of Constitution of India'.

In the decade, that followed imposition of emergency and various Constitutional Amendments rendered the Constitution completely in a chaos. But most of the damage was undone by 1980. It appears that due to this reason the book was not printed again and abandoned. I was given a copy of this book in 1988 or so as to have an overview or bird eye view of the Constitution as a law student without going through the bulk of Constitutional provisions. Even though very little needed to revise the book, the proverbial circle is never complete as knowledge gets outdated far earlier than we notice.

The book proceeds with 1707 when death of last Mughal Emporer Aurangzeb gave East India Company an opportunity to start taking over pieces of India by entering into treaties and subsequently also by using force. The federal nature of India, however is also a historic fact. A King would rise from one small King and attempt to command whole of continent, through provinces of smaller Kings. Though few were successful. Skandgupta of Gupta Dynasty was the King which had largest area of Indian Continent under his command. His rein extended to present day NWFP in Pakistan to Myanmar.

I rediscovered the book in my library, some time back, while usual reshuffle and on search, I found that the book was not  available in any form anywhere. For the benefit of my daughter who is a Law Student, I revised this book and have now decided to share with other students of law or political science who may need to understand the core issues of Constitution of India before delving further into the subject. I would strongly recommend this book to every person interested in the subject.

This book shall also be of help to any person who wants to understand the structure of governance in India besides its evolution.
